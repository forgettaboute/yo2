Information on the United States Olympic Committee

The United States Olympic Committee (USOC) is a nonprofit organization that serves as the National Olympic Committee (NOC) for the United States. As the NOC, the USOC is responsible for selecting, training, and sending athletes to represent the United States at the Olympic Games, as well as at other international multi-sport competitions, such as the Paralympic Games, Pan American Games, and Youth Olympic Games. The USOC also works to promote and develop Olympic and Paralympic sports in the United States, through funding, sponsorship, and support for national governing bodies (NGBs) and other grassroots organizations. The USOC was founded in 1894 as the American Olympic Association, and was recognized by the International Olympic Committee (IOC) as the official NOC for the United States in 1921. Since then, the USOC has played a pivotal role in the success of American athletes on the world stage, from the likes of Jesse Owens and Wilma Rudolph in the early 20th century to more recent stars like Michael Phelps and Simone Biles. Along the way, the USOC has also been instrumental in promoting amateur sports and creating opportunities for athletes to compete at the highest level, regardless of their background or financial resources.

At the heart of the USOC's mission is a commitment to excellence, both in athletic performance and personal character. The organization recognizes that the pursuit of athletic excellence can also help develop important life skills, such as discipline, perseverance, and teamwork. As a result, the USOC is dedicated to fostering a culture of respect, integrity, and teamwork among athletes and all members of the Olympic and Paralympic communities. To achieve this goal, the USOC offers a wide range of programs and initiatives that go beyond traditional sports training and competition. These include athlete career and education services, which help athletes prepare for life beyond sports by providing education and career counseling; diversity and inclusion initiatives, which aim to create a more diverse and equitable Olympic community by promoting inclusion and addressing issues of discrimination and inequality; community outreach programs, which help promote the values of the Olympic movement by engaging with local communities and promoting healthy lifestyles and social responsibility; and the Team USA Ambassador Program, which allows retired athletes to continue to represent and promote the United States and the Olympic movement through public speaking engagements, appearances, and other outreach activities. Through these programs and initiatives, the USOC seeks to inspire the next generation of athletes and to promote the Olympic values of friendship, respect, and excellence around the world. By supporting and empowering athletes to achieve their full potential both on and off the field of play, the USOC continues to be a vital and inspiring institution in the world of sports.
